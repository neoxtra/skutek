### FemtoDAQ Vireo 2-Channel Digitizer: Compact, Powerful, Accessible

The **FemtoDAQ Vireo** is our compact, low-cost, dual-channel data acquisition system designed specifically for nuclear physics research and education. Running on an embedded Linux single-board computer, Vireo brings the exact same professional-grade digitization capabilities used at large facilities to researchers and educators who need reliable, high-performance DAQ without the complexity or cost of larger systems.

### Chickadee 32-Channel Digitizer

*Multi-Channel DAQ Systems — Scalable High-Performance Digitization*

![SkuTek Chickadee 32-Channel Digitizer, real-time DAQ demonstration at APS DNP conference](/files/CHK_demo_btpqdj)

*SkuTek Chickadee 32-Channel Digitizer, real-time data acquisition demonstration at APS DNP conference.*

**32 Channels per module:**

- 14-bit resolution at 100 MHz sampling across all 32 channels.
- Seamless scaling from 32 to thousands of channels.
- LEMO coaxial inputs (HPGe detectors) or FERA ribbon connectors for detector arrays.

**Clock and timestamp synchronization:**

- Facility clock is received by the module and used for ADC sampling.
- Timestamps can be restarted on demand and synchronized to other facility electronics.

**Channel synchronization:**

- GPS or White Rabbit clock synchronization for multi-module systems.
- VME backplane trigger distribution across all digitizers in a crate.

**High-Speed data streaming:**

- 1 Gbps per module (default) and optional 10 Gbps streaming.
- Both triggered and triggerless modes of operation.
- Triggered: Real-time event selection reduces data volume.
- Triggerless: every pulse waveform is streamed; no data is lost.

**FPGA-powered signal processing:**

- Built-in pulse height, shape analysis, and timestamp extraction.
- Configurable trigger logic with multiplicity counting.
- Sub-nanosecond timing resolution for fast scintillators and precision coincidence measurements.

**Universal detector compatibility:**

- From high resolution HPGe and silicon detectors to classic scintillators: CsI(Tl), NaI(Tl), LYSO, BC400.
- Versions of this electronics powered the liquid noble gas LZ detector.

**Open software ecosystem:**

- Open-source Python and C libraries for custom data acquisition.
- GRETA-compatible event format for seamless integration.
- Debian Linux OS enables network file systems and automated workflows.

**Proven in high-performance applications:**

- 1,000+ channels deployed for the LUX-ZEPLIN (LZ) dark matter experiment, one of the world's most sensitive physics detectors.
- Operating continuously for the past 5 years with zero reported errors since installation.
- Trusted for mission-critical data acquisition.

**VME module format:**

- VME card format helps with system building.
- VME crate provides power to the modules.
- VME data plane is not used for data transfer.
- Module readout and control proceeds via Ethernet.
- Readout proceeds individually and in parallel for each module.

### Demonstrating Compton Scattering Through Coincidence Detection

**Developed for student labs and classrooms.**

Compton scattering experiments provide fundamental insights into gamma-ray interactions with matter and serve as excellent demonstrations of coincidence measurement techniques. Using the FemtoDAQ Vireo's dual-channel coincidence capabilities, we demonstrated Compton scattering between two detectors and visualized results in real time.

## Coming Next Month

**Cosmic ray detection results:** Results from our cosmic ray experiment, where we detected, recorded and analyzed cosmic muon rays decaying within a detector.

- We screened and captured our muon decay events using a customized Python script, which is now a feature in our open source software.
- Performed analysis on the event files to extract the electron emission from the muon decay.
- Calculated the half-life and mean-life of muon which had substantial agreement with the theoretical value.
